⚡ Quick Wins

Do These
This Week

These cost nothing and some will boost your score immediately. No excuses — do every single one.

  • 1Electoral Register — register to vote at your current address at gov.uk/register-to-vote. This is the single biggest quick boost on your credit file. Takes 5 minutes. Free.
  • 2Report Your Rent — use Credit Ladder or Canopy to add your rent payments to your credit file. You've been paying rent — that history should count. Free.
  • 3Check for Errors — go through all three free reports (Experian, Credit Karma, Clearscore). Dispute anything wrong. Old address? Wrong employer? Paid-off debt still showing? Get it fixed.
  • 4Close Unused Accounts — old store cards, old bank accounts you forgot about. Tidy your file. Unused credit can look risky to lenders.
  • 5Don't Apply for Lots of Credit at Once — every hard search shows on your file. Space applications out. Too many in a short time looks desperate.
  • 6Keep Utilisation Low — if you have a credit card with a £500 limit, try to keep the balance under £150 (30%). High utilisation tanks your score even if you pay it off.
  • 7Pay Everything On Time — set up direct debits for minimum payments on everything. One missed payment can stay on your file for 6 years.

🏦 Banks & Overdrafts

Get a Real
Bank Account

A bank account with a small overdraft is a foundation. Even if you never use the overdraft — having it and not using it helps your file.

🏦

Monzo or Starling — Start Here

Both do a soft check only — no hard search on your file. You get a full UK bank account, sort code and account number. Once you've used it well for 6 months, apply to upgrade to one with an arranged overdraft.

✅ No credit check to open
📈

Small Arranged Overdraft

Once you have a bank account history, apply for a small arranged overdraft — £100 to £250. Don't use it. Having an unused arranged overdraft shows lenders you can be trusted with credit. It's a signal, not a spending tool.

💡 Have it. Don't use it.
🗓️

Pay Your Bills From It

Set up your direct debits through your main account — rent (via Credit Ladder), energy, broadband, phone. Regular payments in and out tell lenders you're reliable. Stability on an account is worth more than a high score with no history.

📊 Consistent activity = healthy file

Time Is the Main Ingredient

Credit history length matters hugely. A 3-year-old account with consistent payments beats a new account every time. Don't close your oldest account. Don't keep switching banks. Settle in one place and let time do the work.

⏱️ Oldest account = most valuable
🚫 Avoid This

Things That
Wreck It

Some of these are obvious. Some aren't. All of them will damage your file for years.

🚨

Payday Loans

Even if you repay them on time, having a payday loan on your file tells mortgage lenders you were in financial difficulty. Many lenders auto-reject anyone who has used payday loans in the last 2–3 years. Avoid entirely.

❌ Auto-rejection trigger for mortgages
🚨

Missing Payments

One missed payment stays on your file for 6 years. Set up direct debits for the minimum on everything — even if you plan to pay more manually. The DD is a safety net.

❌ 6 years of damage for one mistake
🚨

Too Many Applications

Each hard search leaves a mark. Applying for 5 credit cards in a week looks desperate. Use eligibility checkers first — they use soft searches and don't affect your score.

❌ Use eligibility checkers — not applications
🚨

Credit Repair Scams

"We can fix your credit in 30 days for £299." No they can't. Legitimate errors can be removed free — you do it yourself via the credit agencies. Anyone charging to "fix" your credit file is a scam.

❌ Never pay anyone to fix your credit
