Why This Works

When rent is the problem, solve rent first

Most people try to fix money problems by earning slightly more. But if rent is eating 50–70% of your income, earning slightly more doesn't fix anything. A live-in job changes the whole equation — accommodation becomes part of your pay package, so almost everything you earn is yours to keep.

Getting One

How to actually land a live-in job

The fastest route

1
Decide which type fits you. Pub work if you're sociable and can handle late nights. Care work if you're patient and caring. Hotel if you want seasonal flexibility.
2
For pub/hotel: walk in, don't email. Show up in person between 2–5pm (after lunch rush, before evening). Ask for the manager. "I'm looking for live-in work, available immediately."
3
For care work: contact 3–4 agencies today. Tell them you're available for live-in placements and can start within 2 weeks. DBS check takes time — start it now.
4
Ask the right questions upfront. Is the room private? Are bills included? What's the notice period to leave if needed? Don't find out after you've moved in.
5
Get it in writing. Accommodation terms, notice periods, what's included. A verbal agreement isn't enough when it's also your home.
⚠️ Know your rights

If your employer also provides your accommodation, they can deduct rent from your wage — but they cannot take you below National Minimum Wage after deductions. Get the accommodation terms in your contract before starting. If you're dismissed, you may also lose your home — so always have a backup plan.

Benefits Impact

Starting a live-in job may affect Universal Credit, Housing Benefit, or Council Tax Reduction. Report any change of circumstances to DWP immediately. Your in-work benefits may change but you should still be better off overall — use entitledto.co.uk to calculate.
