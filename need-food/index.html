Free Apps

Get food from your phone — right now

These apps put free or nearly-free food in your hands. Most people have never heard of them. Download all of them today.

🟢
OLIO
Free food and stuff from neighbours
100% Free

OLIO is a sharing app where neighbours and local shops post food they'd otherwise throw away — completely free. Bread, veg, tins, cooked food, snacks. You request it, you collect it. Some areas are busier than others but it's worth having on your phone.

Local shops and supermarkets also post surplus stock through OLIO Food Waste Heroes — this is how Tom got loads of food from his local area, including enough to give some away.

olioapp.com → App Store → Google Play →
💡 Got food you're not going to use? Post it on OLIO too — what goes around comes around, and your neighbours will remember you.
🥡
Too Good To Go
Restaurant and cafe surplus — £2–£5 a bag
From £2

Restaurants, cafes, bakeries, supermarkets sell their end-of-day surplus in "magic bags" for £2–£5. You pick up at a set time (usually closing). A £3 bag from Greggs, Pret, or a local restaurant can easily be worth £10–£15 of food.

Not 100% free — but genuinely cheap. A family can eat well for £5–£10 using this app regularly. Brilliant if you have even a small budget.

toogoodtogo.com → App Store → Google Play →
💡 Set notifications for your favourite spots — bags go fast. Bakeries at end of day are the best value. Greggs bags are legendary.

Freecycle / Freegle
Free stuff from locals — including food
100% Free

Freecycle and Freegle are community groups where people give away things they don't need — completely free. Food comes up regularly (tinned goods, pantry clearouts, bulk buys people can't finish). Also great for household items, furniture, clothes.

freecycle.org → ilovefreegle.org →
💡 Join your local Facebook group too — "Free Stuff [Your Town]" — people post food and household items constantly.

Karma
Surplus meals from restaurants — up to 50% off
Up to 50% off

Similar to Too Good To Go — restaurants sell surplus meals at up to 50% off through Karma. More restaurant-focused, good in cities. Worth having alongside TGTG as some businesses are on one but not the other.

karma.life →

Oddbox, Wonky Veg, and similar services deliver imperfect (but perfectly edible) fruit and veg rejected by supermarkets. Boxes from £10–£15 deliver a week's worth of fresh veg. Much cheaper than supermarket prices.

Food Bank Delivery — Ask For It

Can't get to a food bank? No car, disability, kids, anxiety? Ask if they deliver. Many Trussell Trust banks and independent banks now offer home delivery — especially post-COVID. It's not always advertised. Ring the number on their page and ask directly: "Do you offer home delivery?"

Getting a Referral Fast

GP, school, JobCentre, Citizens Advice, council — any of these can give you a food bank referral. If you're struggling to get one quickly, call Citizens Advice: 0800 144 8848 (free). They can arrange a referral same day in most areas.

Kids

Free school meals and holiday food

🏫
Free School Meals
Universal (Reception–Y2) + means-tested (Y3+)
Free

All children in Reception, Year 1, and Year 2 get free school meals automatically. From Year 3, it's means-tested — you qualify if you're on UC with income under £7,400, or certain other benefits.

Apply through your council. It's fast, free, and most people who qualify don't bother applying.

Apply on GOV.UK →
💡 Holiday hunger schemes — many councils run free meals for kids during school holidays. Search "[your council] holiday hunger scheme" or ask your child's school.
🥛
Healthy Start Vouchers
Pregnant or have a child under 4?
£442/year

If you're at least 10 weeks pregnant or have a child under 4, and you're on UC, Income Support, or Child Tax Credit — you qualify for Healthy Start. £4.25/week in vouchers for milk, fruit, veg, and infant formula.

healthystart.nhs.uk →

🛒 SOCIAL SUPERMARKETS & COMMUNITY PANTRIES — FREE OR NEARLY FREE FOOD

Social supermarkets let you shop like normal but pay almost nothing — typically £3–£5 membership gets you a trolley of food worth £20–£30+. Community pantries work the same way. They run on supermarket surplus, donations, and food waste redistribution. Most people don't know they exist in their area.

How to find one near you:

  • Search "social supermarket [your town]" or "community pantry [your city]" on Google
  • Ask at your local food bank — they'll know every food resource in the area
  • Check with your council — many fund or host them
  • Look up Community Fridge Network → — free food, no membership, no questions

Examples: Borderlands (Newcastle area), Fare Share community shops, FareShare Go outlets, Your Local Pantry network across UK.
