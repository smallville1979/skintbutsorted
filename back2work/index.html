Real Talk First

This worked during COVID. It still works now.

What Actually Happened

During lockdown the government ran 2–3 week paid training courses — customer service, data skills, digital — and people got paid to attend. Some walked straight into apprenticeships before their course even finished. The bright ones who engaged properly got instant job offers. The courses still exist. They're funded differently now but the principle is the same: free training, employer connections, real jobs at the end. If you went on one then — there are equivalents now. If you didn't — this is the page you needed.

Skills Bootcamps
Free Guaranteed Interview 🔥 Most Valuable
DfE Funded

The single best scheme running right now. 12–16 week intensive courses, completely free, funded by the Department for Education. Employers are involved from day one — many guarantee an interview on completion. Sectors include digital, construction, green energy, HGV driving, healthcare, and more.

Cost
Free to you
Length
12–16 weeks typically
Outcome
Guaranteed interview + qualification
Benefits impact
Usually can still claim UC

Unlike old-style college courses, Bootcamps are employer-led — the companies who will hire you help design the training. That's why the job conversion rate is high.

Apprenticeships — Any Age
Paid From Day 1 Employed + Qualified 🔥 No Age Limit
Employer Funded

Apprenticeships are not just for school leavers. Adults in their 30s, 40s, and 50s do them. You are employed from day one — paid a wage, trained on the job, get a qualification. The minimum apprenticeship wage is low but many employers pay National Minimum Wage or above.

£6.40/hr (April 2024)

NMW or above
Training

Age limit
None — adults welcome

The people who got instant apprenticeships during COVID-era bootcamps didn't get lucky — they engaged properly, showed up every day, asked questions, and made themselves impossible to ignore. That's still the move.

Restart Scheme / Jobcentre Support
Free Funded Activities
DWP

If you've been on Universal Credit for 9+ months and are required to look for work, you may be referred to the Restart Scheme. Mandatory for some, but the support is real — CV help, interview coaching, funded training, employer introductions. Don't wait to be referred — ask your work coach directly what's available in your area.

Who gets it
UC claimants 9+ months
Duration
Up to 12 months support
What's included
CV, training, employer links
How to access
Ask your work coach
💡 Ask your Jobcentre work coach specifically: "What Skills Bootcamps or funded training can I access right now?" — they don't always volunteer this information.

The NHS bank (temporary staffing pool) is particularly useful — work when you want, immediate need, good hourly rates.

The Insider Move

How to get the most out of any scheme

The formula that gets you hired before the course ends

1
Show up. Every single day. Attendance is the first filter. The people who get offers are always the ones who showed up when others didn't.
2
Engage properly. Ask questions. Volunteer for demos. Make yourself visible to the tutors and any employer visitors.
3
Talk to every employer who visits. They're there to scout. Introduce yourself. Ask about their team. Follow up with an email the same day.
4
Don't wait for the course to finish. If an employer shows interest, push for a conversation now. The people who got apprenticeships mid-course didn't wait — they moved fast.
5
Get the reference before you leave. Ask your tutor on week one who gives references and what you need to do to get a strong one. Then do those things.
The Advanced Database Lesson

A bad teacher and a broken computer means nothing if you're the one making connections. The qualification matters less than the relationships and the reference. Engage with the people, not just the content — that's what gets you hired.

⚠️ Benefits while training

Most free government training does not affect Universal Credit. But always check with your work coach before starting. Some schemes count as "work-related activity" and satisfy your UC conditions. Others may need to be declared. Don't assume — ask first.
