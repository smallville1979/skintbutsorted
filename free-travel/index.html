TICKET SPLITTING —
SAME TRAIN. NO CHANGES. HALF THE PRICE.

a ticket office woman showed this trick. completely legal. widely unknown. pass it on.

✂️

💡 HOW IT WORKS

Instead of buying ONE ticket from A to C, you buy TWO tickets: A→B and B→C. Same train. No getting off. No changes. The train stops at B anyway — you just stay sat there.

The way UK train fares work, two split tickets often cost way less than one through ticket. The train operators know this exists. It's not a loophole — it's just how their pricing works.

You stay on the same train. You just have two tickets in your pocket instead of one. That's it. That's the whole trick.

📊 REAL EXAMPLE — NEWCASTLE TO LONDON

Without Splitting

Newcastle → London

With Splitting

£47

Newcastle→York (£22)
York→London (£25)
Same. Train.

📋 THE RULES — KEEP THESE IN MIND

✅ You must be on the train when it passes the split point. If York is your split, ya stay sat on the train as it passes through York. Don't get off.

✅ Both tickets must be valid for the same train. TrainSplit handles this automatically — it only shows valid combinations.

✅ Works with any train operator. LNER, Avanti, GWR, TransPennine, CrossCountry — all of them.

✅ Works with railcards. Apply your railcard to each ticket separately for double savings.

⚠️ Keep all tickets on ya. If checked, show both. The inspector knows what split ticketing is.
