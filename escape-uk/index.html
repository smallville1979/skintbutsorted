Australia — The Easiest Way Out

Age 18–35. Up to 3 years. No sponsor needed. UK citizens get the best deal.

Au Pair Hack: Your Costs Go Near Zero

Live with a family. Free room. Free food (3 meals a day). Earn ~A$200-350/week. Everything you earn is basically profit. Your flatmate did this. It works.

🦘Working Holiday Visa (417)

The classic. Work for any employer up to 6 months at a time. Travel. Stay up to 3 years with the UK-Australia Free Trade Agreement — no farm work required anymore.

Visa cost: A$670 (~£342) Age: 18–35 Need savings: A$5,000 (~£2,500) UK citizens: 3 year option Process time: 2–4 weeks

Minimum wage in Australia is A$24.10/hour (~£12.50). Most casual hospitality and farm work pays more. You will earn more than you spend if you're sensible.

Au Pair — The Actual Cheat Code

Work 25–40 hours a week looking after kids. Get free accommodation, free food, pocket money. Cost of living = almost nothing.

  • What you getFree room and board with a host family. 3 meals a day. A$200–350/week pocket money depending on family and hours.
  • What you doSchool runs, childcare, light housekeeping. Basic English is fine. No childcare qualifications required in most cases.
  • How to find familiesAuPairWorld.net (largest database), Seek.com.au, GumTree Australia. Apply to multiple families at once. Video interview them too — you're choosing them as much as they're choosing you.
  • Red flagsMore than 45 hours/week. No private room. Less than A$150/week. No days off. Walk away.
  • Reality checkSome families are brilliant. Some are nightmare employers. Check reviews. Ask for contact details of their previous au pair. Trust your gut at the interview.

How to Fund the Flight

Getting there is the main barrier. Here's how people do it.

  • 3 months of temp work firstWarehouse, hospitality, care work. £1,000–1,500/month. Live at home or with mates. Save aggressively. 3 months = enough to go.
  • Bank switching bonusesStack free banking sign-up offers — Lloyds £200, Halifax £175, First Direct £175. Legal, takes 30 mins. That's part of your flight money.
  • Universal CreditIf you're currently on UC, you have a 4-week notice period. Plan the timing. Don't just disappear — close the claim properly.
  • FlightsLondon to Sydney — £700–1,000 if you book 6–8 weeks out on Skyscanner. Avoid school holidays. Tuesday/Wednesday flights are cheapest.
  • Realistic total to leaveA$5,000 visa requirement + £700 flight + £200 buffer = about £3,000. 3–4 months of saving at minimum wage. That's it.

What Happens to Your Benefits

The bit everyone worries about. Honest answers only.

Universal Credit

Stops. UC is means-tested and residence-based. It stops the day you leave the UK. Close your claim properly before you go — don't ghost the DWP or you'll have an overpayment debt when you come back.

PIP / DLA (Disability Benefits)

Non-means-tested. You can continue receiving PIP for up to 13 weeks abroad. Australia has a reciprocal social security agreement with the UK — meaning some benefits can continue longer. Check GOV.UK or call PIP directly before making plans. Don't assume either way.

GOV.UK — Benefits Abroad

NHS

Australia has a reciprocal healthcare agreement (Medicare). You get access to Australian public hospitals and bulk-billed GPs. Get a Medicare card on arrival. It's free for UK citizens.

Coming Back

You can come back and reclaim UC. There's a Habitual Residence Test — you'll need to demonstrate you're back properly. Give it a few weeks of being back before applying. Your National Insurance record is unaffected by time abroad.
