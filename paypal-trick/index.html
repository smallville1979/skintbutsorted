🤔 What Is This?

A legal, legitimate way to create a short-term float when you are temporarily skint.

PayPal Credit is a credit line inside your PayPal account. When approved, you can move that balance into Revolut as real spendable cash instantly. You have days or weeks to use it for food or essentials, then repay before interest is charged.

Think of it as an interest-free short-term loan you arrange yourself in minutes. No calls. No paperwork. It only works if you repay in full before interest kicks in. Miss that date and it becomes 23.9% APR. This is a bridge for people with income coming in — not a fix for people already in debt.

📋 Step by Step

Takes about 10 minutes to set up the first time.

Step 1

📱 Get PayPal Credit Approved

Open the PayPal app or paypal.com. Find "PayPal Credit" under Products or Wallet. Apply — usually approved instantly. Limits typically range from £100 to £1,500+.

Does not need a perfect credit score. PayPal is more lenient than most banks. If declined, improve your file first — see the credit guide.

Use Revolut to pay for food, bills, Too Good To Go, ASDA — whatever you need. Works anywhere Mastercard is accepted.

Step 5 — Critical

⏰ Repay PayPal Credit IN FULL Before Interest Hits

PayPal Credit has an interest-free period. Check your own account for the exact due date and set a reminder now. Pay the full balance before that date. Full balance = zero interest. Any balance left over = 23.9% APR immediately on the remaining amount.

To repay: log into PayPal, go to PayPal Credit, make a payment from your bank account. Pay the full outstanding balance — not just the minimum.

✔️ Is This Right for You?

✅ Use This If:

  • You have income coming in and just need to bridge a gap
  • You can repay the full amount before your PayPal Credit due date
  • You do not already have an outstanding PayPal Credit balance
  • You need food or essentials and have no other way right now

❌ Skip This If:

  • You are already in debt and maxed out on cards
  • You cannot guarantee repayment before interest kicks in
  • You want to use it for takeaways or non-essentials
  • You still have an outstanding balance from last time
