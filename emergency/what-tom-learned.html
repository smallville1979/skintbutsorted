What Tom Learned

25 years broke. Here are the lessons that actually stuck.

Asking Directly Works

Tom spent 10 years watching people suffer in silence. Pride. Shame. Fear.

Then he met the Roses guy in London.

The Roses Guy

Homeless. £5. Single tub of Roses chocolates. Stood outside a pub where smokers queue.

"50p a sweet. Better than the shop, yeah?"

He had effort. He had service. He had dignity. People GLADLY bought them. Some bought multiple.

He turned £5 into real cash that week.

Tom's lesson: Direct asking works because it's honest. You're not hidden behind a form. You're not filtered by an algorithm. You're real.

Who to Ask (And How)

  • Family/friends: "I'm in crisis. I need £X. Can you help?" Works better than hints.
  • People with houses: Ask one person you trust. They usually help.
  • Your workplace: Many companies have hardship funds. Ask HR privately.
  • Religious groups: Even if you're not religious, churches/mosques/temples often have emergency cash. No conditions. Just ask.
  • Street asking: £10-30 in an hour in busy areas. Instant feedback. You know it worked.

Stretching Money Is a Skill

Tom learned this from broke people who somehow survived on less than he could imagine.

The Rules That Actually Work

  • Aldi first. Yellow sticker aisle Sunday evenings. Batch cook. £5 can last 2 days.
  • Never shop hungry. You spend 40% more.
  • Buy in bulk (when you can). Rice, beans, flour. Lasts months.
  • Apps over nothing: Too Good To Go (£10 for £2.50), Olio (free food from neighbours), food banks.
  • Freezer is your friend. Bread, milk, leftovers — freeze everything.
  • Check what you're owed. £19 billion in benefits go unclaimed every year. Most broke people are owed more than they think.

Tom's lesson: Stretching money isn't deprivation. It's a skill. And skills get better with practice.

Building Wealth (Even From Nothing)

Tom's biggest surprise: you don't need much to start building.

The Free Money Nobody Claims

Lloyds pays £200 to switch accounts. PayPal gives £10. Revolut pays £70 referral. Stack them all = £280 from signing up.

Tom knows people who've made £1,500 in a year just from signup bonuses + cashback apps.

Not fast money. Not sketchy. Just stuff that exists if you know about it.

The Real Wealth Rules

  • Avoid payday loans (1500% APR). Just never. There's always another way.
  • Credit cards work IF you pay in full. 0% interest, builds your score, free stuff.
  • Build your credit file. Electoral roll register (free). Rent reporting (free tier exists). Takes 6 months but you qualify for better rates.
  • Council hardship funds are real. £500-£2,000. Every council has it. You just have to ask.
  • Your network is your net worth. One person who helps you is worth more than ten apps.

Tom's lesson: Wealth isn't about big moments. It's about knowing what's available and asking.

Why Cyberbegging Fails (And Street Asking Works)

Tom watched for 5 years on Reddit. Real people. Real desperation. Real failure.

What Happens When You Beg Online

  • Trolling: The rejection hits harder online. Invisible. Permanent. Public.
  • Algorithmic rejection: Your post disappears. Nobody sees it.
  • Disbelief: "Prove it." "Why should I help?" The questions are endless.
  • Mental health collapse: The shame of public rejection compounds the original crisis.

Why Street Asking Works Better

  • Instant feedback: You know immediately if it worked.
  • Visible service: The Roses guy sold a product. That's different.
  • Human to human: No algorithm. No troll. Just a person deciding to help.
  • Dignity intact: You asked. They helped. Done. No public shame spiral.

Tom's lesson: Never beg on the internet. Ask someone in person. The conversion rate is better, the mental health impact is healthier, and you get your answer today, not next week.

Community Networks (The Real Safety Net)

Tom learned that broke people help broke people. Better than any charity.

What Actually Works

  • Olio: Download. Free food from neighbours within walking distance. Zero shame. No forms.
  • Food banks: Emergency food in 1 hour. Google "[your area] food bank".
  • Local Facebook groups: "Broke, no food, help?" — real people respond. Real stuff arrives.
  • Community fridges: Free food, 24/7, no questions, in some areas. Ask local cafes.
  • Mutual aid groups: People helping people in your actual neighbourhood. Not charity. Mutual.

Tom's lesson: The best safety net isn't government. It's your community. And community only works if you ask.

The Truth Tom Learned

You will always be broke sometimes. That's life for most people. The difference is: some people know what to do when it happens, and some don't.

Tom built this site so you know what to do.

The moment you ask for help is the moment everything changes.
